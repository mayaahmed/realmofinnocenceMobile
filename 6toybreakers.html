Chapter 6

The Toy Smashers

Tara watched the little boy unwrap his present. It was a battery operated monkey which could clap when a button was pressed. Tara's family was staying at the boy's place. Tara never got such expensive toys. But her parents gifted him this fascinating toy monkey. Tara and her siblings had played with it a little back at home. Tara loved the clapping monkey.

Tara waited for the boy to press the button that made the monkey clap. Instead the boy took the monkey and threw it hard at the floor. The monkey broke to smithereens in a matter of seconds. Tara and her siblings had never seen any kid behave like this. They were clearly shocked. The boy's parents were watching. No one reprimanded him. This was clearly accepted behavior.

After a few days, Tara visited a fun fair with her family. Tara's father bought them a small boat made of recycled metal. The boat would move on water when a candle was lit. This toy was of immense interest when the children got home. They spend hours watching the boat move in a bucket filled with water.

The boy wanted this boat. No one thought of buying him one because of how he behaved with his last gift. Tara and her siblings would not part with their boat. The boy started to wail loudly to the utter amazement of Tara.

"I am tired of all this disturbance because of a stupid boat" said the boy's mother. She then took the boat and threw it on the floor. The boat broke in to many pieces.

"This explains why the kid thinks that toys are for throwing on the floor" exclaimed Tara.
