Chapter 9

Lost

"Can you give this sari to the partis for washing?" asked Tara's mother.

Tara's family was visiting their native place. Partis were washer women of the village. They were washing clothes at the stream. Tara could see the stream from the house. She had also been to the stream a few times before. It should be no problem.

"Sure" said Tara. She took her mother's sari and handed it over to the partis. And she made her way back home.

Tara walked and walked but she could not see the house. Any minute now the house would turn up she thought. But it never did. Tara began to panic. She was lost. After what seemed like an eternity she saw some people.

"I am from Thodupuzha. Can you take me back to Thodupuzha?" she asked the strangers.

"Little girl, this entire town is called Thodupuzha. What is your father's name?"

Tara's father's name was no help. No one really knew him. He had left for the city years back.

"Take me back to Thodupuzha. I am from there" said Tara, now almost on the verge of tears.

"This entire town is called Thodupuzha" said the strangers laughing at the information.

Luckily for Tara, her uncle turned up just then and said "This child is ours."

Tara was safe. She returned home. She felt really silly about getting lost. Tara's mother got many scoldings for sending Tara alone to the stream. Tara was given a lot of information about family name, house name, and emergency contact names, that day.
