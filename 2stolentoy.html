Chapter 2

The stolen toy

Lata arrived at Tara's door. Lata was a couple of years senior and was full of gaming ideas. Tara felt privileged that Lata decided to spend time with her.

"Let us play house-house" said Lata.

Lata had heard about Tara's house-house toy collection. These toys were small household items. Tiny pans, pots, spoons, and ladles. Tara being the only girl child was pampered with plenty of house house toys. Girls loved playing house-house where some girl would play the role of mother. She would then act as though she was cooking and fed others.

"You have plenty of nice toys" said Lata. "Playing with you was fun. I will visit again."

As Lata was leaving she decided to do Tara a favor.

"Remember a few days back Geeta came to play with you?" asked Lata

"Yes" said Tara.

"Well, Geeta stole a pan from you. I saw it at her place" said Lata.

"I did not notice I had anything missing" said Tara.

Tara never cared much about her toys. Her mother took great pleasure in buying her toys all the time. She just shrugged the news away.

"You should go and get it back from her" insisted Lata.

"Let it go. I have plenty" said Tara.

"Can't you see this is stealing? You must do the right thing. Get it back from Geeta. Come with me now" said Lata.

It was late evening. It was clear that Lata loved drama. Tara followed Lata to Geeta's house. Lata rang the door bell. Geeta's mother opened the door.

"Geeta stole a pan from Tara's house the other day. We have come to collect it back" said Lata.

A little later, Geeta came and handed back the toy to Tara. She said nothing and quietly closed the door. There was nothing to say. Lata had betrayed Geeta's trust. Geeta had confided in Lata. She never thought Lata would go about squealing. Moreover, Lata hardly knew Tara. Tara could see Lata felt very good about the entire thing. As they were leaving Tara heard loud scoldings and whimpering. The entire episode was unpleasant for Tara. The irony being that Tara did not care at all about the silly old pan that caused all the trouble.
