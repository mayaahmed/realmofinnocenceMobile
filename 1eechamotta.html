Chapter 1

The fear of Eecha Motta

"Eat fast otherwise eecha motta iddum. "

Tara heard her mother proclaim. Eecha motta iddum meant that the housefly will lay eggs in her plate.

Tara looked at her plate. She wondered whether the housefly got a chance to lay eggs already. How much time passes before the housefly decides to lay eggs on a kids plate? There was no one to ask. Tara was taking no chances. Anything that remotely looked like eggs was going out of her plate.

And as luck would have it, no dish was spared mustard seeds by Tara's mother. Every dish would be tempered with mustard seeds. There was plenty of chance that some of these seeds were Eecha motta (eggs of housefly). Tara would pick out these seeds carefully before putting any morsel of food in her mouth.

"Eat fast otherwise eecha motta iddum" repeated Tara's mother.

It was taking forever for Tara to get rid of the mustard seeds and she decided to just grab a handful of food and eat it. But she was tortured by the fact that she might have eaten a few eecha motta. Meal times became miserable for little Tara.

One fine day Tara saw pictures of housefly eggs at her school in the Biology lab. She was relieved to see that it looked quite different from mustard seeds. Eecha motta was white whereas mustard seeds were black. Suddenly Tara was eating at a speed her mother approved of. Eecha motta bothered her no more.
