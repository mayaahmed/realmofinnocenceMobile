Chapter 5

The Dress Disappointment

Tara got a chance to take part in a church dance. The Dance teacher was a terror. The only person the teacher liked was a wiery little girl everyone called mouse. All the mothers were making dresses for the girls for the dance. Tara's mother spend days adding frills and laces to Tara's dress. Needless to say, Tara's dress was the best. Tara was so looking forward to wear her dress at the dance.

On the day of the dance, the girls and their dresses went to the venue before the mothers. The mouse's mother did not bother to make a dress for her. So the teacher had to rent one for her. The teacher looked at all the dresses and decided to give Tara's dress to the mouse.

"My mother made it for me" protested Tara feebly.

"You can take it back after the dance" said the teacher. The teacher then gave the mouse's dress to Tara. When Tara's mother arrived backstage she was surprised to see Tara wearing a different dress.

"What happened to your dress?" asked Tara's mother.

"The teacher gave it to her" said Tara pointing to the mouse.

"Why did you agree to that?" asked Tara's mother.

"Could I have said no the teacher?" asked Tara.

"Of course you could. But now it is too late to take your dress back. It is time for your show" said Tara's mother.

Tara's mother then went on to fix Tara's makeup. The dress was a misfit for the mouse. It was made frill by frill for Tara. Everyone around sensed Tara's disappointment. They came around Tara and told her how wonderful she looked and cheered her up. Tara was happy to get her dress back after the dance.

"If anything like this ever happens again, I will say no" said Tara to herself.
